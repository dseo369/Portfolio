Overview

Airports around the world send out around 115,000 flights per day. The sheer volume of data necessary to continue operations would be troublesome to say the least without some form of a database backing up all the information. A back-end database would solve many critical problems that would go unnoticed without one such as duplicate passengers on multiple flights or corrupt data. This database will store information such as the different airports and the airlines contained within the flights and their passengers.
